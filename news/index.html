WorthIt.co appoints Conshohocken resident as Chief Technology Officer

The Houston-based service that is redefining online/mobile shopping, WorthIt.co, appoints Conshohocken resident and systems engineer and TorSearch founder, Chris MacNaughton, as Chief Technology Officer (CTO) on the one year anniversary of its launch. MacNaughton will direct the continued refinement of the WorthIt.co website and mobile application, polishing the serviceâ€™s shopping process for its rapidly growing member base.

MacNaughton will work in tandem with founder and CEO, Jason Kaminsky, to refine the website and mobile app, bringing the best possible technology to the forefront of the member experience. Kaminsky had previously been working with an outside vendor and is eager to bring the personal touch and commitment of a full-time CTO to the WorthIt.co team.

Prior to joining WorthIt.co, MacNaughton served as a systems engineer at Corporation Service Company, in Wilmington, Delaware, where he oversaw evaluating, planning and deploying application solutions to improve business processes. In addition to deploying, optimizing and maintaining internal .Net and Java applications, he also handled deployments such as Apache Zookeeper and Solr. In his role as CTO for WorthIt.co, MacNaughton will administer technical aspects of both the front and back end of the service and will work to further improve efficiencies leading to a smoother, more precise experience for users.
